Twenty-Third Annual ACM SIGACT-SIGOPS Symposium on
Principles of Distributed Computing
(PODC 2004)

July 25-28, 2004, St. John's, Newfoundland, Canada

PODC is a conference that focuses on research in the theory, design, specification or implementation of distributed systems. PODC is sponsored by ACM SIGACT and SIGOPS.

Special Events

  • This year we have two invited speakers:
    • Ariel Orda: Over Two Decades of Research on Networking Games
    • Doug Lea: Concurrency: From PODC to J2SE, and Back
  • This year we have two tutorials:
    • Joffroy Beauquier: Self-stabilization: Past, Present, Future
    • Ran Canetti: Security and Composition of Cryptographic Protocols
  • Sightseeing tour over Sunday lunch time
  • This year's special track is on Algorithms and Data Structures for the Internet.
  • The Dijkstra Prize in Distributed Computing will be presented.
  • PODC 2004 is collocated with the PODC Workshop on Concurrency and Synchronization in Java Programs; joint submission is encouraged.
  • A selection of the papers is planned to be published by Springer-Verlag's journal Distributed Computing, continuing with a past collaboration.
