ACM SIGMOD/PODS Conference: Vancouver, 2008
Welcome


For decades, the joint ACM SIGMOD/PODS Conference has established itself as the top data management conference in the world for researchers, practitioners, developers, and users to report and share cutting-edge ideas and results, and to exchange techniques, tools, and experiences. In 2008, SIGMOD/PODS was held in Vancouver, in Canada's beautiful British Columbia, from June 9 to 12, 2008.

The highlights of the SIGMOD/PODS program are as follows:

SIGMOD (June 10-12):

  • 3 Keynote Presentations:
    • Ben Shneiderman [University of Maryland]
      Extreme Visualization: Squeezing a Billion Records into a Million Pixels
    • Sridhar Ramaswamy [Google]
      Extreme Data Mining
    • William O'Connell [IBM]
      Extreme Streaming: Business Optimization Driving Algorithmic Challenges
  • 5 Tutorials:
    • Provenance and Scientific Workflows: Challenges and Opportunities (3 hours)
      Speaker(s): Susan Davidson and Juliana Freire
    • Object/Relational Mapping 2008: Hibernate and the Entity Data Model (EDM) (1.5 hours)
      Speaker(s): Betty O'Neil
    • Query Answering Technique on Uncertain and Probabilistic Data (1.5 hours)
      Speaker(s): Jian Pei, Ming Hua, Yufei Tao, Xuemin Lin
    • Information Fusion in Wireless Sensor Networks (1.5 hours)
      Speaker(s): Eduardo Nakamura and Antonio Loureiro
    • Introduction to Recommender Systems (3 hours)
      Speaker(s): Joseph A. Konstan, University of Minnesota
  • 78 Technical Paper Presentations
  • 15 Industrial Paper Presentations
  • 30 Demonstrations
  • 6 Post-conference workshops

PODS (June 9-11):

  • Keynote Speaker: Peter Buneman [University of Edinburgh]
    Topic: Curated Databases
  • Invited Tutorial 1: Effective Characterizations of Tree Logics
    Speaker: Mikolaj Bojanczyk [Warsaw University]
  • Invited Tutorial 2: Dependencies Revisited for Improving Data Quality
    Speaker: Wenfei Fan [University of Edinburgh]
  • 28 Technical Paper Presentations

We hope you have enjoyed SIGMOD/PODS 2008 in Vancouver!
