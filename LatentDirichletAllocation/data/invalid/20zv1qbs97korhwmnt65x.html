Why Do Price Comparison Websites Get a Bad Name Are They Evil?

Posted by admin in Everything Money Related

The humble price comparison website sometimes gets a bad reputation from the financial services industry. Insurers, banks, and other purveyors of financial products and services often rebel against the rise of the comparison website, and there are several providers who actually refuse to associate with any of the aggregator services out of a sense of pride or out of a determination not to contribute to their success, but why do service providers have such disdain for the price comparison industry? Here are some reasons.

It Keeps Prices Down and Prevents Competition

There are several different subsectors when it comes to the provision of financial products and services, but most of these providers operate using the same general marketing tactics and strategies. New customers are almost always preferable to older existing ones in the world of financial service providers, so the best deals are always held out to anyone looking to switch from their existing provider to a new one. One of the reasons behind this is that the advent of the price comparison website has injected much higher levels of competition into the financial product environment than in years past; while it doesn’t always work perfectly, a price comparison website can keep prices low because how many different quotes, drawn from a large number of companies, it can furnish to a prospective customer.

It’s Not Foolproof, but It’s Better Than Nothing

If you compare landlord insurance deals on UK Landlord Insurance.com, you’ll easily see that there can be a very wide variation in the range of insurance cover you can get for roughly the same price. You will see that many insurers will offer landlord insurance packages that only feature a few core pieces of cover, while others will be more exhaustive and provide much higher levels of cover to protect a landlord from any eventuality. These more all-encompassing policies may have higher premium prices, but not always; sometimes you might even find an insurer offering a policy with relatively low cover at a price that is higher than one of its competitors that’s offering more for less! This goes true for almost every financial service provider, whether it be life insurance and car insurance, bank accounts and savings bonds, or business loans and mortgages.

It Exposes Poor Business Practice

The bare fact of the matter is that when a price comparison website feeds a customer several price quotes and one is far-and-above the worst choice by offering the least amount of cover for the highest price, the service provider behind the quote ends up with a proverbial black eye. Comparison sites expose firms that offer poor value for money, and ironically companies that have been exposed in such a way often blame the comparison site for their bad publicity instead of turning their gaze inward to where the true problem lies.

Why You Shouldn’t Be Scared of TENS Therapy

Posted by admin in Health

If you’ve heard of Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation, you’ve probably thought that it sounds like some sort of torture method used on James Bond after he’s captured by a criminal mastermind, but you couldn’t be more wrong about TENS therapy.

It’s Not Just For Supervillains

No, it’s not ripped from the pages of a pulp novel or a comic book – TENS therapy is real, and it’s a widely respected pain management technique. The idea of running an electric current through your body seems to be counter-intuitive when it comes to reducing pain – we’re taught from an early age not to touch an electrical socket, after all – but you’re not getting electrocuted. In fact, TENS therapy doesn’t even hurt.

TENS therapy doesn’t involve getting strapped into a gurney like you’ve seen in the movies. In fact, you simply apply the TENS unit pads, a pair of electrodes, to the portion of your back where you feel the most pain, and then switch on the machine itself. Instantly a low-voltage current will jump from one electrode to the other, and in the process that current will also travel across the nearest cluster of nerves and disrupt the pain signals they routinely send up to your brain. It’s safe, painless, and results in a tingling sensation that many patients find quite pleasant after they get used to it.

It Just Works

TENS therapy isn’t something that’s new or something that only quacks and ‘holistic’ homeopathic medicine men talk their patients into trying; it’s a well-respected therapy and has been in use for years. The reason TENS therapy is used so widely is because it tends to work, and when it does work it does quite well in relieving pain symptoms – especially when it comes to lower back pain.

That isn’t to say that TENS therapy is for everyone. If you’re suffering from back pain because of a neurological condition, using a TENS unit has a higher probability to solve your problems than if you suffered a back injury, but even if you threw your back out picking up a heavy object or if you were in a bad car accident where you suffered whiplash doesn’t mean that using TENS therapy can’t relieve some of your pain as your body goes through the natural healing process. In the end, the only way to know if TENS therapy is for you is to try it, but if you’ve been holding back out because you fear it will be a painful experience or that you’re wary of allowing electricity to course through your body, rest assured that TENS therapy could be the missing piece of the pain management puzzle that you’ve been trying so desperately to find.

The History of the Vaporizer

Posted by admin in Body & Mind

It’s been about fifteen years since the first commercial-grade vaporizers were made available to the public. Nowadays, vaporizers are so small and compact that they can be small enough to fit in a pocket or a handbag, but it wasn’t always that way; here’s how vaporizers have evolved over the years to where they are now.

Great in Theory, but Not Exactly Practical

At first, vaporizers were large and bulky pieces of machinery that featured large, over sized chambers and heating elements and required power from a wall socket in order to operate. The heating element inside the vaporization chamber was large and inefficient; while it reached the needed temperature to release the compounds inside the chamber, it worked on conduction, which tended to add an acrid, unpleasant taste to the vapor it produced, making vaporizers more of an expensive piece of experimental industry than the type of object you would be able to enjoy reliably. The delivery system for the vapor also involved long tubes or even filling balloons with the vapor, making it an ungainly and cumbersome experience, and the sheer bulk of the vaporizers of yesterday made it a trial to transport.

The Wonders of Modern Technology

Today, many of the problems inherent in these first-generation vaporizers have been solved. Vaporizers have become smaller and less obtrusive, and the materials used as a heating block have become refined to the point where convection-based heating could be used, allowing the vapor released to be free of any burned or acrid taste. On top of that, miniaturization technology has progressed to the point where true portability has been achieved, and now websites such as Vapetropolis offer a wide array of truly portable vaporizers that are small enough to slip into a pocket or a handbag.

Portable vaporizers are also much safer and easier to use than their older, bulkier cousins; thanks to newer, more efficient materials and breakthroughs in insulation, portable vaporizers can remain completely cool to the touch even as their internal chambers are blazing hot. Even better is how the modern portable vaporizer is indeed completely wireless, as the same rechargeable battery technology used by mobile phones are used to power the heating element inside. Many portable vaporizers even feature USB chargers like your smart phone, making it easy to keep your vaporizer charged until you need it.

Causes of Heartburn and How to Treat It

Posted by admin in Health

Heartburn can be a mild inconvenient annoyance or it can be a painfully debilitating condition that negatively affects your quality of life. However, before you can treat heartburn, you need to know what can cause it.

An Ounce of Prevention

It’s called heartburn because it feels like there are hot coals right under your breastbone, even though your heart has nothing to do with why you’re feeling like you could breathe fire at any given moment. Instead, most instances of heartburn occur because excess digestive acid has splashed up out of your stomach and into your esophagus, causing pain and irritation to the sensitive tissues there.

The opening of your esophagus that leads to your stomach should in most cases stay nice and closed. There’s a lower esophageal sphincter there, and its job is to keep the esophagus as a one-way road – food goes down and nothing comes back up – but if something weakens that muscle or if it doesn’t close completely, the results could be leaving it exposed to stomach acid.

That’s where the trouble starts. Suddenly you’ve got this burning irritation in your chest and it’s all because you’ve got an open pathway between your esophagus and your stomach. This can be caused by any number of things, but the biggest culprit is your diet: if you’re eating too much or if you’re eating the wrong types of foods, your lower esophageal sphincter relaxes, paving the way for heartburn. Much of preventing heartburn is simply eating more modestly and staying away from foods known to relax that muscle like coffee, tomatoes and alcohol.

A Pound of Cure

Of course once you’ve got heartburn, it’ a bit too late at that point – the horses have all escaped, so it’s little use in closing up the barn now! This is why there are so many medications on the market that are designed to relieve heartburn symptoms.

However, not every heartburn treatment is created equal. For every antacid or proton pump inhibitor on the market, there’s an herbal or natural remedy that purportedly does the same thing. Many of these may not do what they say on the tin, or may only work well at first and lose their efficacy over time; what’s important is to choose the right one for you.

If you seldom suffer from heartburn, a simple antacid may be the best option for you. Antacids neutralize the amount of digestive acid in your stomach and tend to work quickly for isolated incidents. If you’d rather go the natural route, an herbal remedy such as Heartburn No More, which uses apple cider vinegar to naturally balance your stomach acid levels, may be more your speed. Finally if you’re a chronic heartburn sufferer you may need time to allow your esophagus to heal. A proton pump inhibitor, which actually shuts down your stomach acid production, may be your best option for long-term relief.

The Ultimate Guide to Meditation

Posted by admin in Body & Mind

No matter If you want to get rid of stress, gain peace of mind, manifest absolutely anything or lower your blood pressure…meditation can help.

If you are new to meditation and are keen to find out more, then make sure you pay full attention to the important information below…

It all starts with being alone

Make no mistake about, meditation is a solitary activity. If you are trying to meditate with other people around, then you will have trouble reaching your inner state.

For this reason, find a place where you can be alone and not be distracted for a while. Unplug the phone, turn off your cell, and ignore the outside world for a bit.

You then need to get comfortable

In order to meditate properly, you will need to get comfortable. It doesn’t matter if you are sitting or lying down, just make sure your body is comfortable and able to get relaxed. Some people like to lie down on the bed, while others find it easier to get comfortable in a chair.

Decide how long you are going to meditate for

Experiment with different meditation sessions. You will want to find the perfect length for your needs. If you are pressed for time, or you have another engagement in 30 minutes, then make sure you set a timer so you don’t have to keep looking at your watch.

At the end of the day, it doesn’t matter if your meditation session is short or long, as long as you receive some kind of benefit from the session.

Focus on your breath and clear your mind

One of the main complaints about meditation is being unable to get rid of unwanted thoughts. When this happens, you should look to focus on your breathing.

Inhale through your nose deep from the stomach, and then hold it a few seconds before you exhale slowly. Keep doing this until your mind becomes clear and focused.

You should try to meditate on a regular basis

As with anything else in life, the more you meditate, the better you will become. Ideally, you should be able to have a meditation session every day, but this is not always possible.

As a minimum, aim for at least 4 sessions a week, so you don’t allow your mind to get out of shape.

Conclusion

Thanks for reading this ultimate guide to meditation. You might find it hard at first, but stick with it, and in no time at all you will be able to easily meditate your mind into a deeper state.

92% of University/College Students Make These 8 Financial Mistakes!

Posted by admin in Everything Money Related

University should be a fun and exciting time. However, for most students, this is not the case, as they struggle financially and become deep in debt.

If you don’t want this to happen to you while at university, then don’t make the following 8 financial mistakes!

Not shopping online

No matter if you need a new textbook or a cell phone case, you should buy online for the cheapest prices. As an example, i-Adapters.com offer the cheapest cell phone accessories in the industry, so pick yourself up a bargain right away.

Not having a job

Sure, you need to focus on your studies, but that doesn’t mean you can’t get a part time job to improve your finances. Even 10 hours a week will give you a much needed cash injection.

Not using a bike

Do you really need an expensive car, or to use public transportation every day? Instead, buy a second hand bike and you can travel everywhere for free. It will also help you stay in shape.

Not having a budget

You need to know exactly where your money is going every month. It’s all too easy to simply waste money and then wonder where it all went. With this in mind, set a monthly budget and then stick to it.

Not taking advantage of student discounts

One of the main perks of being a student is that many places will offer you a discount. For this reason, do a little research to find out exactly what you are entitled to, and then start saving some money.

Not cooking your meals

Ordering pizzas and visiting the local kebab shop might seem convenient, but is it really the best way to spend your money? Of course not. This is why you should try and cook from scratch as much as possible, so you can save money and eat healthier at the same time.

Partying every night

One of the major mistakes that most university students make is going out every night to party. Sure, this can be a lot of fun, but in the end it will leave you broke, and even deep in debt. This is why you should only go out a couple of nights a week, and when you do go out, make sure you keep an eye on how much you are spending.

Not buying second hand books

Do you really need to buy your books brand new? It’s usually really easy to find second hand books around campus, for a fraction of the price.

6 Common Mistakes When Hiring a Building Contractor

Posted by admin in Property and Homes

As you are no doubt aware, the building industry gets a lot of bad press and negative attention. That’s not to say there aren’t any decent building contractors out there, it’s just that the rogue companies always seem to get all of the headlines.

If you want to avoid hiring a building contractor that will make your life a living nightmare, then you should pay attention to the following 6 common mistakes.

Not checking reviews

These days, it’s extremely easy to check reviews of building contractors by going online. Simply perform a quick search in Google, or visit a site like Contractor Place to find out everything you need to know.

Not asking for references

Any legitimate building contractor should have many different references which they will happily show you. If a contractor doesn’t have any references, then this is a good sign that you should stay well clear and look elsewhere.

Not asking about qualifications

Would you hire a lawyer who has not gone to law school? The same thing applies in the building industry.

These days, there is no excuse for a building contractor not to have some qualifications under their belt. If they don’t have qualifications, then there is a good chance they don’t really know what they are doing, or they are just trying to make a quick buck.

Not getting a written agreement

Quite simply, if you don’t get a written agreement between yourself and the contractor, then you are putting you and your property at great risk.

With this in mind, only hire a building contractor that is happy to put everything in writing. This includes the price, the work to be carried out, and the time-frame in which the job should be completed.

Not checking for insurance

In many States, it is required by law that building contractors have liability insurance. This means that should they cause damage to your property, then you will be covered. Hire a building contractor without liability insurance, and you could be in for a whole world of trouble. Don’t take the risk.

Not getting a written guarantee

If you buy a product from the store and it’s faulty, then you can take it back for a replacement or refund. The same principle should apply in the building industry. Always get a written guarantee from the contractor, so if the job is not to your liking, they can’t just leave you high and dry.
