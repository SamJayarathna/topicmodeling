Twenty-Second ACM Symposium on 
Principles of Distributed Computing 
(PODC 2003)
July 13-16, 2003, Boston, Massachusetts

PODC is a conference that focuses on research in the theory, design, specification or implementation of distributed systems. PODC is sponsored by SIGACT and SIGOPS.

PODC 2003 is supported by generous contributions by the Hewlett-Packard Company, IBM Research, the Microsoft Corporation, Sun Microsystems Laboratories.

SPECIAL EVENTS

  • There will be an election this year for chair of the PODC Steering Committee.
  • There will be an election this year for member at-large of the PODC Steering Committee.
  • Invited speakers:
    • Ross Anderson, University of Cambridge
    • Michael Franklin, University of California at Berkeley
    • Butler Lampson, Microsoft Research
    • Silvio Micali, Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • In honor of his sixtieth birthday, PODC will feature a series of lectures illustrating and celebrating the impact of the work of Michael Fischer by
    • Leslie Lamport, Microsoft
    • Nancy Lynch, Massachusetts Institute of Technology
    • Albert Meyer, Massachusetts Institute of Technology
    • Rebecca Wright, Stevens Institute of Technology
    This lecture series is being organized by Nancy Lynch of MIT.
  • Pre-conference tutorials on July 13:
    • Incentives and Internet Computation by Joan Feigenbaum and Scott Shenker
    • Content Protection Technologies by Jeffrey B. Lotspiech, Tushar Chandra, and Donald E. Leake Jr.
    PODC tutorials are free of charge.
  • Sunday night will feature a social event hosted by Sun Microsystems Laboratories!
  • The first  Dijkstra Prize in Distributed Computing will be presented.  Click here for the award's call for nominations.
  • Security in Distributed Computing Special Track, chaired by Amir Herzberg. Submissions on any topic in the intersection of security and distributed computing are solicited.
  • A selection of the papers is planned to be published by Springer-Verlag's journal Distributed Computing, continuing with a past collaboration.
