Special Interest Group for Restorative Health

Developed in 2001, the Special Interest Group for Restorative Health, or SIGIR for short, is composed of concerned health professionals, doctors and nutritionists that are passionate about helping Americans overcome obesity and its associated weight problems. Healthy and sustained weight loss is the primary goal, but naturally, that includes dealing with things like diabetes, heart disease and other weight-related health problems.

Although Americans are more active than in previous years, it isn’t helping reduce their waistlines overall. Research recently published shows that in over two-thirds of U.S. Counties, people have become more active over the past ten years. But as a whole, Americans are still very much overweight. There’s clearly a crisis at hand that the nation in general isn’t making progress on – neither on a personal level or government level.

Diet is a Key Factor

Many of our expert panel members are nutritionists. The American Medical Association believes that diet is the number one contributing factor to the epidemic. This is untrue of other industrialized countries. Nutritionists and health experts both believe that changing your diet to a healthy one can have more of a positive impact on your weight than if you were to just start exercising more.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) says that over a third of the adults living in the U.S. are overweight. An alarming number of children are obese, too. That number comes in at roughly 17%. And it comes at a hefty cost: Americans who live in the worst-performing areas of the country have a lower life expectancy than those of people living in Sudan or Vietnam. That should be an alarming fact to open your eyes.

Life expectancies in the United States stagnated for women in 1,405 counties and for men in 154 counties between 1985 and 2010, researchers reported. And Americans living in the worst-performing counties in the nation, in Appalachia, now live shorter lives than people in Vietnam, Yemen and Sudan.

Food and Brand Lab released its research which proves that making small, simple changes to your diet can have huge benefits – including substituting weight loss that doesn’t come right back. Sounds easy, but the challenge is determining the exact changes that best suit an individual and ones that they’ll be able to stick to.

Magical Diet Supplements

There’s an never-ending stream of weight loss supplements like garcinia cambogia that flood the market each and every year. It can be extremely difficult to know which ones will give you any benefit at all and which ones will work for your situation. The best thing to do is do your research. Don’t go buy green coffee extract just because you seen it on TV or see an internet advertisement.

There are plenty of companies out there who happily produce ineffective supplements and are happy to take your money and run. Yes, some of the natural supplements have definitely been proven to promote healthy weight loss. But they’ll always work best with a sensible diet and healthy lifestyle.

At SIGIR, we particularly like ones that cater to specific needs. i.e. emotional eating & cravings, hormone imbalances, appetite control.
