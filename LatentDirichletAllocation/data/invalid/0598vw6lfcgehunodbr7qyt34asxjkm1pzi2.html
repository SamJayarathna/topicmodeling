Potential Causes of Drug Addiction

Categories:

Diseases & Conditions

by admin

There is no one definitive cause of drug addiction. For this reason, it is usually a combination of things that turn a person using drugs into an addict. There are four agreed upon components of addiction. There is a bio-chemical component, a genetic component, a mental health component and an environmental component.

The bio-chemical aspect is caused by a chemical imbalance in the brain and nervous system, which causes a person to be more susceptible to addiction. The genetic cause, while identifiable, is not well understood. All that is truly known about it is that it causes a predisposition to addiction. Also, the least identified, but probably the most prevalent, is the mental health issue. There are usually environmental issues involved as well.

Regarding the bio-chemical component, a person’s brain has certain chemicals that make the person feel happy or content. Certain drugs, such as heroin, marijuana, pain killers and tranquilizers, can “trick” the brain into producing these feelings. It causes a person to desire more and to keep using the drug, resulting in a drug addiction. After addiction, the user should take into consideration attending a drug rehab in their area for treatment. For instance, if the addict lives in the city of Tampa, he or she should look for an effective drug rehab center Tampa offers depending upon health insurance plans.

Is There a Cure for HPV?

Categories:

Diseases & Conditions

by admin

HPV affects millions of people across the globe with over 30% of all cases transmitted through sexual intercourse. The transmissions usually cause genital warts and/or abnormal cellular changes in genital areas, which may culminate into cancer. Yet, there is no specific HPV cure. The good news, however, is that many times the symptoms clear on their own without application of any HPV cure. The CDC (Center for Disease Control) reports that more 90% of all HPV infections clear on their own. Nonetheless, experts are divided on whether the body’s immune system clears the virus completely or whether the virus is just reduced to levels that cannot be detected.

While there is no cure for HPV currently available, there are treatments that can eliminate the symptoms of the infection. In addition, there are new and effective HPV vaccines that reduce the rates of infection by the virus. Some of the symptoms usually targeted by HPV treatments include genital warts (that are often related to low-risk HPV types which never result in cancers) and the pre-cancerous symptoms that are linked to high-risk HPV types.

Overview of a Low Residue Diet

Categories:

Diet

by admin

A diet low in fiber is commonly referred to as being a low residue diet. Residue is any food substance, including fiber, which bypasses body digestion and lingers in the colon. A low fiber diet restricts these food substances and, in turn, restricts the quantity of undigested materials that are allowed passage through the colon.

A low fiber diet may be prescribed in certain situations. Examples of these circumstances include constriction of the bowel as a result of an inflammatory ailment or a tumor, following bowel surgery, or radiation therapy that causes damage or pain to the gastrointestinal tract.
